About Us

Welcome to the Clare McCabe research group website. Professor McCabe is the Professor in the Department of Chemical Engineering and Chemistry, Associate Dean of the Graduate School, and Director of the Office of Postdoctoral Affairs at Vanderbilt University in Nashville, TN. McCabe also directs the Multicscale Modeling and Simulation (MuMS) group at Vanderbilt, an interdisciplinary research facility housing faculty from the School of Engineering, and works in Vanderbilt's Institute for Nanoscale Science and Engineering (VINSE) group. Her research interests include molecular simulation, theoretical chemistry, computational structural biochemistry, chemical biology, and biophysical chemistry.

Since joining the Vanderbilt faculty in 2004, McCabe displayed excellence as a mentor and supervised students in their undergraduate research projects. At a 2011 faculty awards ceremony, Chancellor Nicholas S. Zeppos presented her with the Madison Sarratt Prize for Excellence in Undergraduate Teaching. McCabe directs a Graduate Assistance in Areas of National Need (GAANN) program at Vanderbilt University in distributing funds from a U.S. Department of Education grant to fund graduate fellowships in the area of advanced materials. Throughout the years, McCabe has published in notable journals, including the American Chemical Society, American Physical Society, CRC press, and The Royal Society of Chemistry.

Why Molecular Simulations?

Speed

Minutes to hours of server time versus hours to days of laboratory experiments. Plus, the simulation can be rewound, fastforward, and replayed at any time after completion with dynamic visualization on a user-friendly, multi-platform interface.

Cost

Budgets can only stretch so far and a pure biological compound such as cholesterol costs roughly $45/gram. In facility computer, several thousands of those molecules can be simulated for only a few cents for every hour of power/bandwidth.

Availability

Record your simulation experiment and share with any collaborator over email, google drive, or whatever. In a different country or continent but still want to run your simulation? SSH into your computer and overcome that physical barrier!
